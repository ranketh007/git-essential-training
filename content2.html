SDG 14 - Life Below Water

Sustainable Development Goal 14 focuses on conserving and sustainably using oceans, seas and marine resources. Oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface and play a vital role in regulating climate, providing food, and supporting biodiversity. However, human activities continue to threaten marine ecosystems worldwide.

Key Facts About Oceans

  • Over 3 billion people depend on marine and coastal biodiversity for their livelihoods.
  • Oceans absorb about 30% of carbon dioxide produced by humans.
  • More than 800 marine species are affected by plastic pollution.
  • About 90% of global fish stocks are fully exploited or overfished.
  • Coral reefs support 25% of all marine species.

Major Threats to Marine Life

The oceans face several major threats that affect marine ecosystems:

  • Plastic Pollution: Millions of tons of plastic enter oceans each year.
  • Overfishing: Unsustainable fishing reduces fish populations.
  • Climate Change: Rising temperatures affect marine biodiversity.
  • Ocean Acidification: Increased CO₂ weakens coral reefs.
  • Habitat Destruction: Coastal development damages marine habitats.

Why Protecting Oceans Matters

Healthy oceans provide food, regulate climate, support biodiversity, and create economic opportunities. Marine ecosystems also protect coastlines from storms and erosion. Protecting oceans ensures sustainable development for future generations.
